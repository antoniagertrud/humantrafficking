What Is Human Trafficking?

Human trafficking is modern-day slavery and involves the use of force, fraud, or coercion to obtain some type of labor or commercial sex act.

Who Does it?

The people who are in control of the trade trade of humans other, otherwise know as the "traffickers" can be thought of as the middle man in the proccess of human trafficking. From tirckery to kidnapping, these people will do what ever it takes to make a 'transaction' of their people.

Who Does It Affect?

Every year, millions of men, women, and children are trafficked in countries around the world, including the United States. It is estimated that human trafficking generates profit of about $32 billion every year. profit per year, second only to drug trafficking as the most profitable form of transnational crime. Human trafficking is a hidden crime this is because victims rarely come forward to seek help because of language barriers, fear of the traffickers, and/or fear of law enforcement.

How Do The Victims Initially get involved?

Traffickers will look for people who are susceptible for a variety of reasons, including psychological or emotional vulnerability, economic hardship, lack of a social safety net, natural disasters, or political instability. They will use these as weak points to get people to think that they are going to be able to help them selves and there family, but in reality these people will most likly never see any compenation for their work.
